The Chef

Where the kitchen
has no walls.

Born and raised in Eastern Washington, our chef grew up along the coulees and wheat fields that define this corner of the Northwest. He traded the line cook's apron for a cast-iron skillet, a pack mule, and a camera — and never looked back.

Every menu is built from what the land and season offer: freshly harvested game, river-caught fish, foraged greens, and the hardwood smoke of open-fire cooking. It's food that earns its place at the table.

Trained in Pacific Northwest & Mountain West culinary traditions
Expert in open-fire, Dutch oven & camp-kitchen cooking

How We Work

Field Philosophy

Three things we
never compromise.

I

Ingredients that belong to the land

Every menu is sourced with the region in mind. Local ranchers, Pacific Northwest fisheries, foraged seasonals, and ethically harvested game are the foundation — not an afterthought.

II

Logistics you don't have to think about

Remote means remote. We pack in, set up, serve, and pack out without leaving a trace. You focus on the hunt, the river, or the guests. We handle everything else.

III

Photographs worth framing

We don't produce stock imagery. Our field photography is honest, character-driven work — the kind that ends up on a wall, in a magazine, or as the defining image of a place you love.
